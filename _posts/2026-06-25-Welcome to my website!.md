I'm a young researcher (well, at least I think I am) from China, studying <b>topology-guided dynamic stimuli-responsive framework material design</b>.
	</p>

	<p>
So this is my website, <em><b>Crease in Frame</b></em>. Actually, it would be more like a collection of memorable works, and would involve a lot of my personal thoughts and study notes. Hopefully, this site can be of help for you.
	</p>

	<p>
As you have seen at the home page, this website will focus on the topic <em><b>Creases, Connections, and Sensing</b></em>, which is also my researcher philosophy. This topic can be explained as three parts:
	</p>

	<p><b>· Topology-guided:  </b>unraveling the mathematical and physical mechanisms behind the design and structural change of chemical devices</p>
	<p><b>· Intelligent:  </b>(1) focusing on stimuli-responsive frameworks; (2) integrating with computational chemistry; (3) exploring wavelet analysis of the wave function to understand electronic behaviours</p>
	<p><b>· Dynamic:  </b>focusing on the unique properties brought by dynamic structural changes, and developing responsive nanoscale devices</p>

</div>


<div style="font-size: 1.3rem;">

	<p><b><em>
Just as a crease alters the morphology and structural locking of a sheet, I am fascinated by how subtle topological changes of frameworks can trigger macroscopic intelligence and truly benefit our life.
	</em></b></p>
